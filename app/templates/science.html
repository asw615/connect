Ethical guide

The well-being of students is an important and sensitive topic. Therefore, it is crucial to approach the use of Connected with the utmost respect for their privacy and feelings. To ensure that the students have a positive experience while filling out the questionnaire, we have created this ethical guide.


Confidentiality: Ensure that students' responses are kept confidential, and that their privacy is respected at all times. Let students know that their answers will not be shared with anyone without their consent.

Privacy: Allow the students to fill out the questionnaire in whichever fashion that suits them the best. Perhaps let them fill it out at home or let them sit by themselves in school. It can be harmful to be forced to sit next to another student while filling out questionnaires regarding well-being and social relations.

Patience: Students should not be rushed when answering the questionnaire, as this can lead to ignorance when answering the questions which can ultimately result in a misleading impression of the social dynamics. It is better to let them take their time with the questionnaire and in turn have well-considered answers.

Support: Provide support for students who may feel distressed or overwhelmed during or after the questionnaire. Let students know that they can approach teachers or school counselors for support or guidance.
